It would also make sense to title this article “expand image”, “fullscreen-image”, “view-in-detail”, "..." all would work; and all these options may be someone's search. This is how to make your sales glamour shot occupy the whole of the viewer's attention. Please also see zoomable-widget for amendable grids of images, or extended-modal-popover. Requirements

  • To render an image or text in a widget, and apply standard UI niceties;
  • To support a full-screen and high resolution sales “glamour-shot mode”;
  • To have normal interaction features (esc or click away etc to close);

History (skippable)
  1. This is split into decades, to be shorter, but events and tech roll-out do not match decade boundaries. Note, a computer life-cycle is about three years (JS frameworks, work laptops, etc), so a decade of a “human-time” is a long time. A very long time ago (1990 era) the internet was too slow for casual images. Screen resolutions where 300-800 pixels across.
  2. In about 2000, the internet used to be slower than today, and desktop based. Desktop screens had more colours than before, and a higher resolution (say 1024 across). Looking at images on the internet was a bigger user journey (I'm not talking about pron). As less people had digital cameras so there was less images available. The user journey “look at my image gallery” held interest for less technical users. Secondly digital cameras were less high resolution for all but the really expensive ones. Thirdly there was less advanced browsers and related technology. So early web-developers used to make a gallery of small faster loading images, with links to click for the bigger version of the same images.
  3. By about 2010, everyone in the EU had DSL, a larger screen again and a 'smart' mobile. A current viewer would just call that “my phone” or similar. Smart phones often have a camera, leading to a proliferation of images being created. Interest in personal image galleries was gone, as everyone had moved to social media. It was much easier to have images hosted on the SM companies servers. Companies had image galleries for their products, as the faster internet meant net-shopping was practical. There was expanded infrastructure (like HTTP 1.1 pipelining), and better caching. JS was widely used to make more complex features. A JS library called jQuery was used widely. Screen sizes had climbed to 1800 pixels across on desktops, 1200 for laptops, and 600 for mobile. One could buy a large expensive Mac with a resolution of 5000 pixels across.
  4. By 2020, phones were released with bigger screens and had better CPUs, and general performance. Doing most internet activity via your phone was common, outside of office-work activities. Internet speeds have increased again, and everyone uses streaming media. The JS libraries that are used for SPA architecture are common (React, Vue, Angular). Technology has expanded with market size and maturity again, HTTP2 and HTTPS are used for everything, and most assets are held in a CDN or Edge network like Cloudflare. Good Apps use images to fit the screen-size of the device being used, via various approaches.
