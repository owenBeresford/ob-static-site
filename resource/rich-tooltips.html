Rich tooltips description

  • A passive response to hovering on outbound links, that will show an “interstitial” before a click happens. This supplies extra data on-top of a cold Harvard notation reference mark.
  • Includes an alternative view for small devices, as an annotated list of links at the end of the document instead.

Why?

  • Harvard notation for links is less good accessibility for web, as it has little context. My articles use many references as I lean-on others authority rather than my own.
  • At the point I implemented the first edition, articles hosted on science direct had this rich link scheme. I thought the interaction was a god experience and copied it.
  • It does suppress memes with 'never gunna give you up' Youtube links. Gen-Z etc: it's a random 80s pop song that I have never heard, as I am too young, but some people who previously used dial-up think it is cool to spam it.

Experience notes

  • This feature supplies annotation about links before users click through them. As my articles are technically focused, and a user may or may not be interested in the link, adding annotation improves understanding on destination.
  • I have conducted UI tests and some interviews on the presence of the popup.
  • As ever, the dev makes a poor user until they stop developing. The external view is important.
  • The popups do temporarily hide the content behind, but they do not receive any input events.
  • A view of the age of the linked content is valuable, and the best decision point available here.
  • Matching the source of my idea, I was hoping for a list of authors on the links. This idea only works sometimes, as authors are only commonly listed in some places.
  • If your situation makes categorical statements about some domain types, this is a faster view so you will waste less time (e.g. there was prejudice about wiki previously).
  • A marketing person would be thinking about adverts.
  • Having the meta data as a separate file enables processes about references, like stats on references.
  • Having this meta data and maintaining it gives me a process to update older possibly expired links, so keeping my content more useful.
  • The link building process does improve my references, as I need to do the academic process on the references.
