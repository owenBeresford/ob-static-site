This is background research into the marketplace for analytics tools.

For the purpose of this article, an analytics tool is one that doesn't create data; but does cache it, and supply the ability to summarise, regress the data, perform trend analysis etc in a variety of fashions, graphical and numerical. Technically the caching step can be skipped is there enough I/O performance; but most systems do cache to be able to organise the data better for reading.

  • The most widely used analytics tool is google analytics. This is free for small volume use, and improves visibility on what website users are doing on that site. I have written in more detail, as a previous CTO had never used it.
