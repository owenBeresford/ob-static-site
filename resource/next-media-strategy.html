Actions/ conclusion

Obviously no-one in the past could have hired you or me as we are today. What do we do to make content in our platforms findable (the point of me doing the below research)?

  • For everything before 2000, the best you could do was add your URL to the end on your paid placement, as outbound marketing. Its expensive, and not very precise or reliable.
  • There are still two basic approaches: post a ‚Äúinline ad‚Äù/ advertorial/ attention seeking message and make it interesting enough; or buy a paid ad. If I am an armchair seller, I can talk about chairs on my website, so people searching for ‚Äúchair things‚Äù are more likely to skim my site. As a non-stupid chair seller, I understand that most people will not randomly buy chairs on a whim, or a celebrity telling them to.
  • If I am good with design/ cartoons, I may be able to make a meme which highlights something relevant, and then mentions the armchairs. This sliding into online conversations is widely used and widely ignored by casual internet people. By 2022, this is Very hit and miss, unless you skillfully make a 'funny message' as first priority, and are funny. Most people aren't very funny 1. The images are normally called info-graphics.
  • I can set up a long series of posts on a platform to try to force the No√∂sphere 2 to include my products (equivalent to 'The Coca cola company' saying that everyone drinks coke every Xmas, and that is what all the small children are excited about). This costs lots of money and time, but distorting general ideas gives you a secure market place (referencing Coke again, historically people didn't drink cold sweet fizzy drinks very much; 'the coca cola company' is a market-maker). This is business studies and outside of the scope of this article. I have used the legal name for the company according to US dividend statements to SEC.
  • I can setup a large scale coupon process; in a completely different market sector (traditionally cereals) to sell the idea of defraying the expensive cost of randomly buying armchairs; by buying excess cereals. In a more modern market; I could sell the coupons in ‚Äúinternet minutes‚Äù cards for PAYG mobile-internet; or prizes for supermarket loyalty card points.
  • I could fund a cultural event/ product, and use the marketing for the event to promote my company and products. A historical example of this was presenting tobacco advertising at football and auto-racing events. Not cheap. A more modern version would be DLC for a popular game, say Fortnite.
  • Chairs aren't the best product fit with Amazon; however I can sell armchairs to people who browse Amazon, and the social search feature. This has the impact of reducing the margin to my business per unit by quite a large amount.
  • I can persuade (via a different company) people to install an app; that logs GPS locations, and supplies adverts relevant to the locations. With price manipulation, the adverts can include discounts that just sell products at a lower markup, similar to ‚Äúcheap shops‚Äù sale rates. See foursquare.
  • For various market sectors, people search in a search engine (classically google); search in pinterest; search in Tiktok. The references below lists how to put information into your website, so any time the first two products interact with it, the user gets a clear ~ hopefully persuasive ~ message. A process that the US citizens do is to ideate ‚Äúthings I could buy‚Äù as a hobby. They permanently look at advertising to get ideas for buying things; regardless of need.
